## Design Context

### Users

Five distinct audiences:

1. **Program managers** — Run grant programs, review applications, distribute funds, track progress. Power users who spend extended time in the platform managing workflows.
1. **Application reviewers** — Login to the app to review applications, comment/discuss and interact with applicants and approve/reject applications
1. **Milestone reviewers** — Login to app to review milestones, comment/discuss and interact with grantees and verify/reject milestones.
2. **Applicants & grantees** — Apply for grants, post milestone updates, receive funds. Task-oriented, need clarity on what's expected and what's next.
3. **Public / community** — Browse funded projects, view impact and progress. Discovery-oriented, need to quickly understand what's happening across an ecosystem.

### Brand Personality

**Modern. Intuitive. Transparent.**

The interface should feel trustworthy and opinionated — confident in its choices, not hedging with generic defaults. Karma has a point of view about how grant funding should work.

### Emotional Goals

- **Trust** — This handles real money and real accountability. The UI should feel solid and reliable.
- **Life** — Not corporate-sterile. Subtle color, warmth, and personality. The platform has energy without being loud.
- **Clarity** — Complex workflows (applications, milestones, fund distribution) should feel simple and navigable.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Subtle color with life** — Not black-and-white minimalism, not neon maximalism. A warm, lived-in palette with the teal brand as an anchor but room for supporting hues that add depth.
- **Seeds layer is deprecated** — The organic/blob/glassmorphism direction is being removed. Don't reference or build on it.
- **Inter is negotiable** — Open to more distinctive typography that reinforces the "modern, opinionated" personality.
- **Light and dark mode** — Both supported, both first-class.
- **Whitelabel constraint** — Many design tokens are runtime-overridable per tenant. Core patterns must work across different brand colors.

### Anti-References

- Generic black-and-white SaaS dashboards (too sterile)
- Overly playful/gamified Web3 aesthetics (not serious enough)
- Corporate enterprise software (too heavy, too gray)

### Design Principles

1. **Opinionated defaults** — Make strong choices. Don't present 5 options when 1 is clearly right.
2. **Progressive complexity** — Simple surface, depth on demand. Program managers get power; applicants get clarity; public gets glanceability.
3. **Trustworthy warmth** — Professional enough for million-dollar grants, human enough that builders feel welcome.
4. **Subtle distinction** — Stand out through craft and care, not through noise and decoration.
5. **Whitelabel-aware** — Every design decision must work when the brand teal becomes Optimism red or Polygon purple.

## Sub-context: AI-Readiness Scanner (`/scanner`, `/s/[slug]`, `/scanner/scans/[id]`)

The scanner is a distinct surface within gap-app-v2 with a different brief than the rest of the platform. Use the rules below for any file under `app/scanner/`, `app/s/`, `app/api/scanner/`, `app/settings/api-keys/scanner/`, or `src/features/scanner/`. Defer to the platform-wide rules above for everything else.

### Users (scanner)

Nonprofit staff (executive directors, comms leads, web managers). They arrive from a Karma blog post, social share, or direct link. Most use a browser; few are technical. They use the scorecard partly as a diagnostic ("am I OK?") and partly as a shareable asset ("look what we got").

Job to be done: "Tell me — quickly, credibly, in language I trust — whether AI agents can find, understand, and donate to my organization, and what I should fix first."

Emotional goals: **credibility** (third-party verdict), **calm authority** ("here's what we measured"), **viral pride** when the score is good.

### Brand Personality (scanner)

Three words: **measured, instrument-like, generous**.

Reference: ora.ai/score/. Anti-references: glassy gradient dashboards, "AI startup" cyan-on-dark, charity-rating badge/shield trust patterns.

### Aesthetic Direction (scanner)

**Instrument scorecard.** Light theme only — scorecards are shared publicly where light reads more credibly.

- **Hero**: radial score gauge as the center of gravity, A–F letter inside, `nn / 100` underneath. Gauge fill is brand teal `#2ed1a8`, track is a tinted near-white. Animates 0 → score on first paint (one ~700ms reveal, ease-out-quint).
- **Categories**: 5 horizontal bars, monospace numerics on the right (`14 / 20`), category names left-aligned, 1-line summary underneath.
- **Typography pairing**: **Inter** for prose (existing), **Spectral** for the gauge headline (existing), **JetBrains Mono** for measurement labels and per-check evidence. The mono-for-numbers / sans-for-prose rhythm is what makes the page feel like an instrument.
- **Color**: brand teal `#2ed1a8` is the only accent. Reds reserved for critical-failure gates (no-EIN). No gradients on text, no glassmorphism.
- **Layout**: single column, max ~760px reading width, generous breathing room around the hero gauge. Detail view stays in the same column but adds an evidence rail underneath fixes.
- **Motion**: one purposeful page-load reveal in the first ~700ms; nothing after. No bounce easing.

### Scanner Design Principles

1. **The number is the brand.** Whatever's on screen, the score and grade have the visual gravity. Strip anything that competes with them.
2. **Numbers wear monospace, prose wears Inter.** Measurements (`14 / 20`, `+5 pts`, `EIN 94-1234567`) are mono. Explanation is sans. This rhythm is the instrument feel.
3. **Teal earns its place.** Reserve `#2ed1a8` for the gauge fill, primary CTAs, and active states. Visual interest from spacing and weight contrast, not color.
4. **Critical state is unmistakable but not alarming.** Surface "no EIN → Trust = 0" inline at the category. Tint, don't shout.
5. **One page-load reveal, then quiet.** All motion in the first ~700ms. After that, the page is still — the user is reading.
