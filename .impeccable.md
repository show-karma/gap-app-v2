## Design Context

### Users

Three distinct audiences:

1. **Program managers** — Run grant programs, review applications, distribute funds, track progress. Power users who spend extended time in the platform managing workflows.
2. **Applicants & grantees** — Apply for grants, post milestone updates, receive funds. Task-oriented, need clarity on what's expected and what's next.
3. **Public / community** — Browse funded projects, view impact and progress. Discovery-oriented, need to quickly understand what's happening across an ecosystem.

### Brand Personality

**Modern. Intuitive. Transparent.**

The interface should feel trustworthy and opinionated — confident in its choices, not hedging with generic defaults. Karma has a point of view about how grant funding should work.

### Emotional Goals

- **Trust** — This handles real money and real accountability. The UI should feel solid and reliable.
- **Life** — Not corporate-sterile. Subtle color, warmth, and personality. The platform has energy without being loud.
- **Clarity** — Complex workflows (applications, milestones, fund distribution) should feel simple and navigable.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Subtle color with life** — Not black-and-white minimalism, not neon maximalism. A warm, lived-in palette with the teal brand as an anchor but room for supporting hues that add depth.
- **Seeds layer is deprecated** — The organic/blob/glassmorphism direction is being removed. Don't reference or build on it.
- **Inter is negotiable** — Open to more distinctive typography that reinforces the "modern, opinionated" personality.
- **Light and dark mode** — Both supported, both first-class.
- **Whitelabel constraint** — Many design tokens are runtime-overridable per tenant. Core patterns must work across different brand colors.

### Anti-References

- Generic black-and-white SaaS dashboards (too sterile)
- Overly playful/gamified Web3 aesthetics (not serious enough)
- Corporate enterprise software (too heavy, too gray)

### Design Principles

1. **Opinionated defaults** — Make strong choices. Don't present 5 options when 1 is clearly right.
2. **Progressive complexity** — Simple surface, depth on demand. Program managers get power; applicants get clarity; public gets glanceability.
3. **Trustworthy warmth** — Professional enough for million-dollar grants, human enough that builders feel welcome.
4. **Subtle distinction** — Stand out through craft and care, not through noise and decoration.
5. **Whitelabel-aware** — Every design decision must work when the brand teal becomes Optimism red or Polygon purple.
