---
name: Homepage copy preferences
description: User preferences for homepage marketing copy - no emdashes, avoid "without team" language, be realistic about automation claims
type: feedback
---

1. Don't use emdashes (—) anywhere in marketing copy.
2. Avoid "without the team" or "replaces the team" language. Instead say "with a lean team" or "without adding headcount."
3. Don't overclaim automation. Programs don't "run themselves." Say "with very little attention" or "minimal oversight" instead.
4. Be realistic about what the software does vs. what humans still need to do.

**Why:** The user (founder) knows the product well and doesn't want to overpromise. Foundations and grant orgs are skeptical of "it does everything" claims. Credibility matters more than hype.

**How to apply:** When writing any marketing or landing page copy for Karma, use measured language. The product automates operational work so lean teams can do more, not replace humans entirely.
